Coffee Brewing Methods ☕

Discover the art and science of brewing the perfect cup

Drip Coffee

Drip coffee is brewed by pouring hot water over ground coffee, which passes through a filter into a pot or mug, producing a clean, well-balanced cup. This method is perfect for those who enjoy a classic, straightforward coffee experience with consistent results.

Espresso

Espresso is made by forcing pressurized hot water through finely-ground coffee, resulting in a small, concentrated shot with rich flavor and crema. The foundation of many beloved coffee drinks, espresso delivers an intense and complex flavor profile that's perfect for true coffee enthusiasts.

French Press

The French Press method involves steeping coarsely ground coffee in hot water, then separating the grounds with a metal plunger. This immersion brewing method produces a full-bodied cup with rich, robust flavors and a distinctive texture that coffee connoisseurs love.

Pour Over

Pour Over brewing allows the barista to manually pour hot water over coffee grounds, creating a precise and clean-tasting cup. This method highlights the subtle flavors and aromatic compounds in your coffee beans, making it ideal for single-origin coffees.

Cold Brew

Cold brew is made by steeping coffee grounds in cold water for 12 to 24 hours, resulting in a smooth, less acidic drink. Perfect for hot summer days, cold brew offers a refreshing and mellow coffee experience with naturally sweet undertones.
